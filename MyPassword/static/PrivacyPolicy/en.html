My Passwords Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026

My Passwords is designed around local-first storage. We do not upload your vault content, master password, decoy password, unlock pattern, or biometric data to servers operated by us.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to the My Passwords iOS app and related website pages.

2. Local Vault Data

You may save accounts, passwords, keys, tokens, identity information, categories, names, notes, and expiration dates. These items are stored in the app sandbox and local device storage.

  • We cannot view your master password.
  • If you forget your master password and do not have a usable backup, we cannot recover your local content.
  • Uninstalling the app may remove local data. Please back up important content first.

3. Permissions

  • Camera: used to scan backup and restore QR codes.
  • Notifications: used for local reminders when saved items are nearing expiration.
  • Biometrics: handled by iOS system authentication. We do not receive face or fingerprint data.
  • Tracking permission: used, if you allow it, to show more relevant ads and measure ad performance.

4. Backup and Restore

QR backup and restore are initiated by you for device-to-device migration. Show migration QR codes only in trusted environments.

5. Third-Party Services

The app may use Google AdMob, the Google Mobile Ads SDK, and Google's User Messaging Platform to show ads, manage advertising consent, and measure ad performance. Depending on your consent, system settings, and SDK behavior, third-party services may process device identifiers, advertising identifiers, IP address, app interactions, diagnostics, approximate location, and ad measurement data.

Ads do not upload your vault content, master password, decoy password, or unlock pattern to us or to ad providers.

6. Feedback and Contact

If you contact us by email, the email address, screenshots, and descriptions you choose to send may be used to respond and troubleshoot. Please do not send your master password or full sensitive passwords in feedback.

7. Data Deletion

You can delete individual saved items inside the app. Uninstalling the app usually deletes local data stored in the app sandbox. Because we do not keep a server copy of your vault, there is no remote vault copy for us to delete.

8. Contact Us

For privacy questions, contact: MyPasswordBook@outlook.com.
